
# Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on Employee Records Dataset


## Project Overview

This project focuses on performing Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on an Employee Records Dataset using Python. The objective is to understand employee demographics, salary patterns, departmental distribution, and workforce trends through statistical analysis and data visualization techniques. EDA helps identify hidden patterns, relationships, and anomalies that support data-driven decision-making.

## Business Objectives

To explore and understand the structure of the employee dataset.

To analyze employee age and salary distributions.

To identify trends across departments, countries, and job positions.

To detect outliers and unusual salary patterns.

To examine relationships between employee age and salary.

To summarize key findings using visual and statistical analysis.

## Tools and Technologies Used

Python

Pandas

NumPy

Matplotlib

Seaborn

Google Colab / Jupyter Notebook
## Dataset Describtion

The dataset contains employee-related information including:

Employee ID

Employee Name

Age

Country

Department

Position

Salary

Joining Date
## Data Exploration

Dataset Information

The dataset was examined using the .info() function to understand the data types, number of records, and missing values.

Statistical Summary

The .describe() function was used to generate summary statistics such as mean, minimum, maximum, and standard deviation for numerical columns.

Categorical Analysis

The .value_counts() function was used to analyze employee distribution across departments, countries, and positions.



## Visualizations and Observations

1. Age Distribution (Histogram)

Observation:
The employee age distribution shows that most employees fall within the middle-age category. The workforce consists of employees from different age groups, indicating a diverse employee population.

2. Salary Distribution (Histogram)

Observation:
Most employees earn salaries within a moderate range, while a few employees receive significantly higher salaries. This indicates variation in compensation levels across positions.

3. Salary Outlier Analysis (Box Plot)

Observation:
The box plot reveals potential salary outliers. A small number of employees receive substantially higher salaries than the majority of employees.

4. Age vs Salary Relationship (Scatter Plot)

Observation:
A positive relationship is observed between age and salary. Older employees generally tend to earn higher salaries, suggesting that experience may influence compensation.

5. Department-wise Employee Distribution (Count Plot)

Observation:
Some departments contain more employees than others. This helps identify departments with larger workforce requirements.

6. Country-wise Employee Distribution (Count Plot)

Observation:
Employees are distributed across multiple countries, indicating an international workforce. Certain countries have a higher concentration of employees.

7. Correlation Heatmap

Observation:
The heatmap indicates the relationship between numerical variables. Age and salary show a positive correlation, suggesting that salary tends to increase with age and experience.

8. Pair Plot

Observation:
The pair plot provides a visual overview of relationships among numerical variables and confirms patterns identified in previous analyses.
## Summary of Findings

The dataset contains employee demographic and salary information.

Employee ages vary across a broad range, creating a diverse workforce.

Salary distribution is uneven, with a few high-earning employees.

Certain departments have a larger number of employees.

Employees are distributed across multiple countries.

A positive relationship exists between age and salary.

Salary outliers were identified through box plot analysis.

Correlation analysis suggests that experience may contribute to higher salaries.
